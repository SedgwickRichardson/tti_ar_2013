Our Power Equipment business comprising of power tools, outdoor products, and accessories delivered HK$18.6 billion in sales, an increase of 9.6% over the prior year. The business accounted for 70.5% of Group turnover in 2010. The business segment result before restructuring grew by 35.2%. This strong performance was driven by continued successful expansion of our lithium-ion cordless platforms, new ranges of consumer tools, innovative accessory programs, and growth momentum from new outdoor product categories.
Industrialopen
In North America, strong momentum in Milwaukee's expanding M12 and M18 lithium ion platforms continued as it captured market share in a rebounding professional power tools market in 2010. In early 2011 Milwaukee launched its high performance next generation Red Lithium' battery technology that delivers 40% more run time, 20% more speed, 20% more torque and unmatched performance in harsh winter environments. Red Lithium is an exciting move to maintain Milwaukee's leadership position in technology and reinforce its position as the premier professional power tools brand. Red Lithium will be carried in all markets across all cordless platforms. Milwaukee is also realizing traction with product specific applications for new channels in Pluming, Automotive and Farm Channels such as the M12™ Grease Gun, in Automotive and Farm, and M12™ and M18™ Pex Expanders in the Plumbing Channel. Innovative products introduced in the period included the M12™heated jacket, ratchets including the most compact cordless ratchet on the market, and multi-tool, a multi -purpose cutting tool. The M12 range added a further seven products in 2010 and has over 30 tools and the M18 range now comprises 25 tools, making it the most comprehensive 18V platform in the professional industry. Europe had strong growth in its key categories of M12, M14 and M18 power tools and added a new M28 platform in 2010 which was strongly received. Milwaukee built on its leadership position in hammers, percussion drilling and demolition equipment with the successful launch of the Mini Mungo, a range of corded hammer drill products. The successful launch of new products in the drilling category was a notable success, a heritage category where Milwaukee holds the number one position. Strong gains were made in the Holesaw market where gap fillers helped secure Bi-METAL Holesaw business at key retail partners. Across the business accessories are now established as an important strategic growth driver for incremental sales and distribution. The accessories program was expanded to include Cordless Red Ramp, Cordless Red Strut, alongside the highly successful RED RACK distribution platform. New categories such as Test and Measurement have further developed the Milwaukee business within Electrical Channels. Despite challenging conditions encouraging growth was seen in the Nordic, Italy and GALP ( check). Australia was a stand out performer for Milwaukee with high double-digit growth as it made strong market share gains.An important new product line was hand tools which gained further listings and traction with partners and was well received by end-users. The hand tool line included, screw drivers, saws, hammers, and is set to double in 2011.
Consumer, Trade and Professionalopen
Consumer power equipment sales stabilized as both RYOBI and RIDGID(confirm if mention Ridgid?) brands achieved market share gains in their respective markets. In North America RYOBI continues to consolidate its market leadership in the DIY segment by offering the most cost effective and comprehensive cordless power tool system for DIY home repairs and renovation. The RYOBI ONE+ system offering also had strong momentum in international markets supported by greater distribution and aggressive marketing. In Europe there is now a well established management team and distribution infrastructure in place which is enabling faster growth. Sales grew X% and momentum has been underpinned by the addition of a significant number of retailer and dealer channels in the period. Strong gains were made in adding new distribution, particularly in Nordic markets and through newly launched categories driving gains in electrical channels. Our AEG brand gained a 30% market share of the cordless market in Sweden in 2010. The brand revitalization at AEG continued leading to footholds being established across all markets, as the brand received strong support from partners. We are encouraged by the launch of the new AEG 12V Multi-tool cordless platform which generated strong sales in the Q4 2010. The Multi Tool offers a range of job site solutions on a single consul platform, thereby offering tremendous convenience, value and efficiency to users.
Rest of the Worldopen
Ryobi achieved solid growth in all international markets led by new product introductions under its new 'hyper green' range of product. A Complete revamp of it corded products was also well received by retailers. In the UK the introduction of new One + bays in key channels positively impacted sales. Australia had strong double-digit growth as the Ryobi brand benefited from new product introductions and entry into new categories. Particular attention was taken to tightly managing inventory to offset the pressure of a strong Australian currency. Outdoor Productsopen
Outdoor Products & Accessories delivered X digit-growth led by new categories, continued strength in Homelite electric products and Ryobi One + Lithium-ion cordless Outdoor Products. A successful re-entry into the cordless market with Ryobi 18V lithium ion outdoor tools delivered gains in sales and product listing. Add in cordless element… but having said that it is all 2011…. Our innovative, lightweight 4-cycle engine that powers Ryobi trimmers and wheeled products with energy efficient performance continues to perform well. 48V Mowers, Gas Trimmers and Ryobi pressure washers all made gains. Our new product line-up supported by aggressive marketing is helping to drive incremental listings with profitable accessories in all categories. Homelite Electric products in core categories of trimmers, mowers and gas powered chain saws targeted at the value segment were strong performers. There was also encouraging growth in Homelite's gas powered generators.
